CP3501 Deep Learning - Assessment 2

Animal Image Classification — A comprehensive study of 10 deep learning architectures with hyperparameter tuning, data augmentation, and ensemble methods.

Project Overview

This project tackles a 7‑class animal image classification task using a variety of state‑of‑the‑art deep learning models. We systematically explored data preprocessing, augmentation (MixUp, TTA, RandomErasing), and hyperparameter tuning (learning rates, epochs, label smoothing) to maximise generalisation and minimise log loss. The work was structured across five tasks, from baseline training to final ensemble prediction.

Models Tested

We evaluated 10 different architectures (Task 3) and selected the top 5 for deeper experimentation:

  • beit_base_patch16_224 — Vision Transformer (ViT) with patch‑based self‑attention.
  • ResNet101 — 101‑layer CNN with residual connections.
  • DenseNet121 — Densely connected CNN for efficient feature reuse.
  • ResNet34 — Medium‑sized residual network (baseline).
  • ResNet50 — Popular 50‑layer CNN with good trade‑off.
  • (Others: DINO, MobileNetV3, EfficientNetB0, ConvNeXt Tiny, EfficientNetB3)

Hyperparameters & Techniques

  • Epochs — 5–10, with EarlyStopping for some runs.
  • Augmentations — Random flips, rotation (up to 15°), zoom (1.2–1.5), lighting, RandomErasing, MixUp (disabled/enabled).
  • Label Smoothing — Softened labels to reduce overconfidence.
  • Test Time Augmentation (TTA) — Averaged predictions over multiple augmented views.
  • Learning Rates — Explored 1e‑3, 5e‑4, 3e‑5, and automatic fine‑tune defaults.

Best Results (Log Loss)

  • beit_base_patch16_224 — 1.2476 (final setup: 10 epochs, lr=5e‑4, TTA, moderate aug)
  • ResNet101 — 1.3891 (6 epochs, TTA, label smoothing, no MixUp)
  • DenseNet121 — 1.4956 (6 epochs, lr=1e‑3, no augmentation overkill)
  • ResNet34 — 1.5443 (5 epochs, max_zoom=1.3, label smoothing, TTA)
  • ResNet50 — 1.6262 (augmentation + label smoothing combined)

Ensemble (mean of top 3) — 1.2650, slightly improving over the single best model, demonstrating the benefit of combining diverse representations.

Conclusion

Our experiments confirmed that careful tuning of augmentations, learning rates, and regularisation (label smoothing) is critical for achieving strong generalisation. While MixUp sometimes hurt performance, TTA consistently improved results. The Vision Transformer (beit) outperformed all CNNs, and ensembling provided a modest boost. Future work could explore more advanced ensembling (stacking) and larger pretrained models.
